43 AD

Roman Londinium

The city of London was founded by the Romans around 43 AD, under the rule of Emperor Claudius. It quickly became an important commercial center and a key outpost of the Roman Empire.

1066

Norman Conquest

London was conquered by William the Conqueror in 1066, following the Battle of Hastings. This event marked the beginning of Norman rule in England and led to the construction of the Tower of London.

1666

Great Fire of London

The Great Fire of London broke out in September 1666 and raged for three days, destroying much of the city. The fire led to significant rebuilding efforts, including the construction of St. Paul's Cathedral.

1851

The Great Exhibition

The Great Exhibition, also known as the Crystal Palace Exhibition, took place in London in 1851. It showcased the industrial and cultural achievements of the time and attracted visitors from around the world.

1940-1941

The Blitz

During World War II, London endured a sustained bombing campaign by the German Luftwaffe known as the Blitz. The city suffered extensive damage, but ultimately recovered and rebuilt after the war.

2012

London Olympics

In 2012, London hosted the Summer Olympics for the third time in history. The event was widely praised for its organization and facilities, and helped to showcase the city as a global hub for sports and culture.
